# CV Tailoring Guide

## How to Tailor Simon's CV for a Specific Job

The goal is to make the CV feel written for this role — not a generic document.

---

## What to Change

### 1. Professional Summary
Rewrite to mirror the JD. Use 1–2 keywords directly from the JD in the first sentence.

**Base summary:**
> Results-driven Applications Developer and IT Solutions Architect with expertise in full-stack development, business automation, and ERP integration. Over 7 years' experience designing, coding, testing, deploying, and supporting business applications for diverse industries.

**Example — Android Developer role:**
> Results-driven Android and Mobile Developer with 7+ years of experience building cross-platform and native mobile applications using Flutter, Dart, and Kotlin. Proven track record delivering production-grade apps for diverse industries, with deep expertise in REST API integration, cloud backends (GCP), and agile delivery.

**Example — Data Analyst role:**
> Data-driven Software Developer and Systems Analyst with 7+ years of experience turning raw business data into actionable insights. Experienced in Power BI, R, SQL, and machine learning models for predictive analytics. Built sales force automation systems with real-time reporting at Nice House of Plastics, enabling data-driven decisions across the organisation.

**Example — IT Manager role:**
> Experienced IT Leader and Software Architect with 7+ years managing technology teams and delivering enterprise-grade solutions. Led full software development lifecycles, ERP integrations, cloud migrations, and cross-functional automation projects. Hands-on background as Head of IT across multiple organisations, with strong documentation and SOP writing skills.

---

## 2. Core Competencies — Reorder by Relevance

Put the skills the JD explicitly mentions FIRST.

**For Android/Mobile role — lead with:**
- Full-stack mobile development (Flutter/Dart, Kotlin, Android, iOS)
- RESTful API Design & Implementation
- Google Cloud Platform
- Unit & Integration Testing (pytest, NUnit)

**For Data Analyst role — lead with:**
- Data Analytics: Power BI, R, SQL, machine learning
- Database: SQL, T-SQL, PL/SQL, Oracle, MS SQL Server
- Automation & Process Optimization
- RESTful API Design & Implementation

**For IT Manager role — lead with:**
- Technical Documentation & SOP Writing
- ERP Integration: Microsoft Dynamics 365 BC, SAP
- Cloud Solutions (GCP)
- Automation & Process Optimization

---

## 3. Work History — Surface Matching Bullets First

Within each job's bullet points, move the ones most relevant to the target role to the top.

**Example: Applying for Android Developer role**
At Mulwana Group, lead with:
- "Developed cross-platform mobile & web apps using Python, Kotlin, and Flutter..."

**Example: Applying for Data Analyst role**
At Nice House of Plastics, lead with:
- "Used Power BI and R for advanced data analytics..."
- "Introduced machine learning models for predictive analysis..."

---

## What NOT to Change
- Job titles — keep accurate
- Company names and dates — keep accurate
- Achievements with numbers — keep the numbers, just reorder/reframe
- Do not invent skills or experience that doesn't exist in the base CV
