## Phase 2 — First Income (First Elastic Payment)

**The 48-Hour Rule — CRITICAL**
When money hits your account, follow this sequence *before* going anywhere or doing anything:

1. **Transfer immediately** → Pay MTN + Viola first (if still outstanding)
2. **Pay arrears** → 2.4M to Standard Chartered (2 months behind)
3. **Set aside school fees** → Move 1M to a separate account if term is near
4. **Grocery run** → Stock food for the month (not eating out)
5. **Only then** → You may spend freely on what's left

> This order exists because your instinct is to go out first. The accounts must be paid *before* you see the money as available.

## Phase 3 — Stabilise (Months 1–3 of Regular Income)

**Target monthly income:** 5,000,000 UGX (baseline from previous salary)

### Budget Allocation (50/30/20 Framework — adapted)

| Bucket | % | Amount (UGX) | What it covers |

| Wants | 20% | 1,000,000 | Going out, girlfriend, family support, impulse |

1. MTN loan (220k) — clear immediately
2. Viola (400k) — clear immediately
3. Standard Chartered arrears (2.4M) — clear within first 2 months of income
4. Standard Chartered remaining balance (~10M) — continue 1.2M/month until done

| Savings account | CBA, Centenary, Stanbic — fixed deposit or savings account | Month 4 onwards |
| Unit Trusts | UAP, ICEA, Britam — invest small amounts monthly | Month 6+ once emergency fund is built |
| Property | Your house — complete it, it's already an asset | When cash flow allows |

### Retirement Target (simplified)
- You have ~32 years to retirement (age 65)

- Start as soon as the emergency fund is in place
