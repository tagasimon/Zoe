# Toastmasters Speech Structure (5-7 min)

## Timing Budget

| Section | Duration | Word Count |
|---------|----------|------------|
| Opening Hook | 30-60 sec | 75-100 words |
| Bridge to Topic | 20-30 sec | 40-60 words |
| Point 1 + Story | 60-90 sec | 150-200 words |
| Point 2 + Story | 60-90 sec | 150-200 words |
| Point 3 + Story (optional) | 60-90 sec | 150-200 words |
| Callback | 20-30 sec | 40-60 words |
| Close | 30-45 sec | 75-100 words |

---

## Section Breakdown

### 1. Opening Hook (30-60 sec)
- Start with impact: bold question, surprising fact, or short funny anecdote
- Do NOT open with "Good evening Mr/Madam Toastmaster" — open with impact FIRST
- Goal: get the room's attention and a smile within 15 seconds
- Then deliver the formal Toastmasters greeting

> Example: "I want you to imagine the worst job interview of your life. Now imagine you showed up 30 minutes late, in pyjamas, with a cat on your lap. That was my first Zoom interview. Good evening, Mr Toastmaster, fellow Toastmasters and honoured guests..."

### 2. Bridge to Topic (20-30 sec)
- Connect the hook to the speech theme
- One clear signpost sentence: "Today I want to talk about..."
- Set the audience's expectation

### 3. Body — 2 or 3 Points with Stories
Each point follows the **STAR format**:
- **S**ituation — set the scene briefly
- **T**ension — what was the problem or stakes?
- **A**ction — what happened or what did you do?
- **R**esult — the outcome + the lesson or punchline

**Humour rule:** Set up an expectation, then subvert it (rule of three works well).
**Transition:** End each point with a punchy one-liner before moving to the next.

> Example transition: "So the lesson I learned that day? Always check your camera is off before you start singing. Always. [PAUSE] Point number two..."

### 4. Callback (20-30 sec) — Optional but powerful
- Reference the opening hook again
- Ties the speech together and usually lands a laugh
- Formula: "Remember what I said at the start about [X]? Well, it turns out..."

### 5. Close (30-45 sec)
- Deliver the core message clearly — one sentence
- End with a memorable final line (not "thank you")
- Then signal you're done: "Mr/Madam Toastmaster."

> Example close: "The next time life hands you a situation that feels impossible, remember: it's probably just the setup. The punchline is coming. Mr Toastmaster."

## Delivery Cue Reference

| Cue | Meaning |
|-----|---------|
| `[PAUSE]` | Stop. Let it land. Count 2 seconds. |
| `[SLOW DOWN]` | This is an important moment — don't rush |
| `[LOOK UP]` | Break from notes, make eye contact with room |
| `[SMILE]` | Signal a light or funny moment |
| `[TIMING: ~Xmin]` | Checkpoint — you should be at roughly X minutes here |

## Pathways-Specific Notes

| Project | Focus |
|---------|-------|
| Icebreaker | Personal story — who you are, 4-6 min |
| Engaging Humour | Humour techniques — rule of three, callbacks, misdirection |
| Persuasive Influence | Clear argument, evidence, emotional appeal |
| Dynamic Leadership | Inspire action, strong close |

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Starting with "Thank you for the opportunity..." — weak opener
- Saying "um" or "uh" as fillers — use silence instead (`[PAUSE]`)
- Reading from notes the whole time — memorise the opening and close at minimum
- Running over time — the red light means stop, finish your sentence and close
- Ending with "Thank you" as the final words — end with impact, then "Mr Toastmaster"
